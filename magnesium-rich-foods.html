Magnesium-Rich Foods Reference

Understanding Magnesium in Your Diet

Magnesium is an essential mineral involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body. It plays a crucial role in muscle and nerve function, blood glucose control, protein synthesis, blood pressure regulation, and bone development.

Additionally, magnesium is vital for DNA maintenance and repair processes, helping to protect against DNA damage that could lead to cancer over time. When magnesium intake is insufficient, research suggests that DNA maintenance functions are often compromised first, as the body prioritizes more immediate physiological needs over these long-term protective mechanisms.

The recommended daily intake for adults is approximately 310-420 mg of magnesium, though individual needs vary based on age, biological sex, and overall health status.

1 Standard Serving = 100mg of Magnesium
